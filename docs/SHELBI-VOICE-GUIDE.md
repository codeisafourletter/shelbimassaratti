# Shelbi Voice Guide

## Source basis

This guide synthesizes:

- Shelbi's side of an SMS history supplied for the project;
- the legacy Plattinum Management Wix site;
- accessible LinkedIn, Snapchat, music-release, and Pink TalkO material;
- the project's verified fact register.

Private messages are used only to understand rhythm and tone. Do not quote, summarize, or expose private conversation content on the public site.

## Core voice

Shelbi sounds:

- direct;
- conversational;
- practical;
- socially confident;
- warm without being sentimental;
- comfortable asking clearly for what she needs;
- more interested in what happened and what comes next than in abstract explanations.

Her public voice adds:

- promotional energy;
- visibility and personality;
- Texas identity;
- music, nightlife, fashion, and media awareness;
- short calls to action.

## Professional translation

The site should sound like Shelbi after she has organized her thoughts and corrected the typing, not like her raw texts were pasted onto a webpage.

Keep:

- plain language;
- first-person ownership;
- quick transitions;
- short openings;
- direct asks;
- natural contractions;
- occasional phrases such as “tap in,” when they genuinely fit.

Clean up:

- typos;
- text-message fragments;
- excessive abbreviations;
- repeated words;
- private slang that would confuse a new visitor;
- reactive or crisis-oriented wording.

## Sentence behavior

Prefer:

- one clear point per sentence;
- a short sentence after a longer explanation;
- concrete nouns and verbs;
- “I help,” “I look for,” “send me,” “here's what I need,” and “this is where I come in”;
- direct contrasts such as “A good song matters. A plan still matters too.”

Avoid:

- “Shelbi leverages extensive experience”;
- “comprehensive artist-development solutions”;
- “her documented history demonstrates”;
- “based on publicly available information”;
- “empowering creatives on their journey”;
- stacked slogans in every section;
- forced caricatures of rap or Southern speech.

## Point of view

Homepage:

- first person for the offer, standards, background, and contact instructions;
- third person only where metadata, a press quotation, or a compact formal biography requires it.

Press kit:

- third-person biography is appropriate;
- facts should remain concise and publication-ready;
- do not fill the page with disclaimers.

## Tone by section

### Hero

Confident and immediate. Say who the site is for and what Shelbi helps move.

### Services

Plain and useful. Explain the work in language an independent artist understands without consultant jargon.

### Artist fit

Selective, not insulting. Challenge fantasy and poor follow-through without sounding like a motivational scold.

### About

Personal and grounded. Explain how management, promotion, events, media, music, and education fit together.

### Track record

Specific and restrained. Use dates, roles, and context where supported. Do not rely on name-dropping.

### Contact

Clear instructions. Tell artists exactly what to send and what not to send.

## Example transformations

Too corporate:

> Shelbi provides comprehensive strategic support designed to optimize independent artists' brand positioning and release outcomes.

Closer:

> I help artists get clear about the record, the rollout, the look, and what needs to happen after the song drops.

Too biographical:

> With more than two decades across multiple areas of entertainment, Shelbi has cultivated a diverse professional portfolio.

Closer:

> I've worked across management, shows, promotion, media, and music for years. I know the creative side, but I also know what falls apart when nobody is handling the details.

Too performative:

> If you ain't ready to grind, don't tap in.

Closer:

> Come with finished music, a real work rate, and enough discipline to follow through.

Too defensive:

> Inclusion of names does not imply a current relationship, endorsement, or individually verified management agreement.

Closer public treatment:

- Label the item accurately by role and date, or leave it out.

## Final voice test

Before shipping, remove or rewrite any sentence that sounds like:

1. a research report;
2. legal protection inserted into normal marketing copy;
3. résumé padding;
4. a generic agency template;
5. fake rapper slang;
6. something Shelbi would be unlikely to say aloud;
7. a claim the evidence does not actually support.
